ACADEMIC APPEAL FORM

Appeals may only be made against a final decision of an academic body and must be submitted within one calendar month of notification of that decision. The appeals process is open to all students of the University of Nottingham, except for those who have had their registration suspended by the University, other than at their own request under the Voluntary Suspension of Registration procedure. 

Before submitting an appeal please read carefully the University’s Academic Appeals Policy and Procedure documents which can be found on the University’s webpages: http://www.nottingham.ac.uk/AcademicServices/CurrentStudents/AcademicAppeals.aspx.
You may also wish to contact the Education Officer in the Students’ Association who will be able to give you help and advice.

  • Complete all sections of the form.
  • Be concise and limit your submission to the facts of the case.
  • You may attach supplementary documentation to support your appeal.
  • If your appeal case is not presented clearly and comprehensibly it may be returned to you to revise before the appeals process can begin.
  • As part of the appeal process, your case may be sent to a representative of the relevant decision making body and to a member of the Academic Appeals Committee so that it can be considered properly. 
  • Experiences with which you are dissatisfied but which do not fit the grounds listed in Section 2 below should not be pursued under the academic appeals procedure.

Section 3 – Student’s summary

  • Please summarise your reason(s) for appealing.
  • Do not exceed 1200 words.
  • The information you provide in this section will be used to make an initial consideration of your appeal.

 

In your summary you should include:

  • details of the decision you wish to appeal against
  • when you received notification of the decision
  • whether or not you have already raised the issue with your School/Department
  • a concise explanation of the circumstances in chronological order, including the key points to support the ground (or grounds) you believe apply.

 

You may attach further details and supporting documentation separately. Please ensure that any additional information is relevant and meets the Policy and Procedure requirements for the presentation of a written case.
