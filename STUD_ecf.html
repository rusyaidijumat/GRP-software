Explanation of Retake/Exam Board:

‘Retake’ means that there would be an opportunity to take the assessment again (ie sit the exam – normally in August/September – or submit new work) as a replacement attempt for the affected attempt.  The affected attempt (and therefore replacement attempt) may be either the initial (‘first sit’) attempt or a reassessment, depending on circumstances.

‘Exam Board’ means that the circumstances that are claimed would be taken into consideration at the Examination Board either in relation to progression to the next stage of the programme and/or in relation to the degree class at the end of the programme.

Apply For Appeal Letter



ACADEMIC APPEAL FORM

Appeals may only be made against a final decision of an academic body and must be submitted within one calendar month of notification of that decision. The appeals process is open to all students of the University of Nottingham, except for those who have had their registration suspended by the University, other than at their own request under the Voluntary Suspension of Registration procedure. 

Before submitting an appeal please read carefully the University’s Academic Appeals Policy and Procedure documents which can be found on the University’s webpages: http://www.nottingham.ac.uk/AcademicServices/CurrentStudents/AcademicAppeals.aspx.
You may also wish to contact the Education Officer in the Students’ Association who will be able to give you help and advice.

  • Complete all sections of the form.
  • Be concise and limit your submission to the facts of the case.
  • You may attach supplementary documentation to support your appeal.
  • If your appeal case is not presented clearly and comprehensibly it may be returned to you to revise before the appeals process can begin.
  • As part of the appeal process, your case may be sent to a representative of the relevant decision making body and to a member of the Academic Appeals Committee so that it can be considered properly. 
  • Experiences with which you are dissatisfied but which do not fit the grounds listed in Section 2 below should not be pursued under the academic appeals procedure.

Section 1 – Reason for the Appeal

  • Please indicate the type of decision that you wish to appeal against.
  • Remember that you may only appeal a final decision, not a provisional outcome.

 

The conditions imposed on progression to the next stage of a taught course or phase of research study. This includes the requirement to take reassessments.

The decision that a student cannot progress to the next stage of a taught course or phase of research study (ie their current programme of study is terminated). This includes students who are not allowed to progress on their current course and who are offered transfer to another taught course or research degree (and so their registration with the University is not terminated).

Classification decision.  This includes appeals against the classification of all undergraduate and postgraduate awards. 

The decision not to award the qualification for which a student is registered.  This relates to decisions made at the end of a student's taught course or research degree; and includes appeals against decisions not to award any qualification or to award a lower qualification (eg a postgraduate Diploma in place of a Masters degree).

The decision of a Fitness to Practise Committee or Practice Assessment Panel. This is relevant to students on professional courses who have been the subject of fitness to practise procedures.

 

Section 2 - Grounds for appeal     

  • To appeal against one of the types of decision listed above, you must be able to demonstrate that one (or more) of the following circumstances apply.

 

Procedural irregularity: A documented assessment procedure was not applied correctly and this disadvantaged the student significantly enough to have materially affected the decision made, rendering it unsound.

If you are appealing on the grounds of procedural irregularity please tell us the name of the documented assessment procedure and where it is published:

 

 

 

 

Manifestly unreasonable: the decision making body took a decision which no reasonable person would find Comprehensible. You must provide substantive argument as to why this is the case.

Prejudice or bias: there  was evidence of prejudice or bias on the part of one or more of the Examiners. You must provide evidence to support this claim.

 


Section 3 – Student’s summary

  • Please summarise your reason(s) for appealing.
  • Do not exceed 1200 words.
  • The information you provide in this section will be used to make an initial consideration of your appeal.

 

In your summary you should include:

  • details of the decision you wish to appeal against
  • when you received notification of the decision
  • whether or not you have already raised the issue with your School/Department
  • a concise explanation of the circumstances in chronological order, including the key points to support the ground (or grounds) you believe apply.

 

You may attach further details and supporting documentation separately. Please ensure that any additional information is relevant and meets the Policy and Procedure requirements for the presentation of a written case.
